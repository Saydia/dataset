This folder contains dataset used for **A Two-Stage Hierchical Framework for Early Detection of Stress and Suicide Risk** thesis project.

# Stress & Suicide Detection Dataset

## Overview
This dataset was collected using a structured Google Form questionnaire for research on stress and suicide risk detection using machine learning and deep learning techniques.

The dataset is part of a two-stage hierarchical framework:
- Stage 1: Stress detection
- Stage 2: Suicide risk classification

---

## Data Collection
The dataset was collected through a structured questionnaire consisting of 24 questions.

A total of **1,436 responses** were collected using a Google Form survey.

---

## Questionnaire Structure

The questionnaire is divided into three sections:

### 1. Demographic Information
- Age  
- Gender  
- Profession  
- District  

### 2. Stress-Related Factors (Q1–Q16)
These questions measure stress-related conditions such as:

- Work pressure  
- Academic pressure  
- Financial stress  
- Job satisfaction (reverse scored)  
- Sleep quality  
- Diet influence on stress  
- Overload of responsibilities  
- Coping ability  
- Fatigue and exhaustion  
- Concentration difficulty  
- Emotional well-being (interest, mood, energy, etc.)

---

### 3. Suicide-Related Factors (Q17–Q20)
These questions assess suicidal ideation and behavior:

- Wish to sleep and not wake up  
- Thoughts of ending life  
- Thinking about methods of suicide  
- Suicide preparation behavior  

---

## Response Scale
All 20 questionnaire items (Q1–Q20) use a 5-point Likert scale:

- 1 = Never  
- 2 = Rarely  
- 3 = Sometimes  
- 4 = Often  
- 5 = Very Often  

---

## Dataset Summary
- Total Questions: 24 (including demographic + structured items)  
- Total Responses: 1,436  
- Data Type: Structured questionnaire dataset  
- Source: Google Form survey  

---

## Purpose
This dataset is used for developing a two-stage machine learning framework for:
- Stress detection (Stage 1)
- Suicide risk classification (Stage 2)
